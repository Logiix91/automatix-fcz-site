# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Operations and IT decision-makers at Dutch logistics, industry, government, and professional-services organizations evaluating an AI-automation partner. They are AI-curious but hype-fatigued: most have already seen generative-AI pilots produce no measurable ROI, so they are actively looking for evidence of real engineering depth before they will take a sales call seriously. The audience is bilingual NL/EN, and includes government-sector visitors for whom compliance posture (AVG/GDPR) is a evaluation criterion, not an afterthought.

## Product Purpose

Automatix FCZ is a Rotterdam-based AI-transformation and intelligent-process-automation consultancy, founded 2026, bootstrapped by two founders with no outside investors. The site's job is to convert a skeptical, hype-fatigued B2B visitor into a booked free consult by demonstrating real engineering rigor (a named 6-phase methodology, concrete pricing model, 8-service portfolio) and an honest account of company size and stage, rather than generic AI-transformation marketing claims. Success looks like booked consult calls and credibility with compliance-minded reviewers (especially in the government and logistics sectors, where AI adoption is lowest and skepticism highest).

## Brand Personality

Technical, blunt. Plainspoken about what AI can and cannot do. Claims are backed by named numbers (CBS/MIT statistics, the 6-phase framework, founding date, team size) rather than adjectives. The site already explicitly rejects hype language ("close the gap between AI hype and AI value," "~95% of organizations see no measurable return on generative AI") and should keep extending that posture into every new section, not just the ones already copy-edited.

## Anti-references

Generic AI/SaaS marketing sites: gradient hero text, tiny uppercase eyebrows above every section, the hero-metric-card template, glossy "AI transformation consultancy" stock-photo sites that could belong to any vendor. Nothing on this site should be mistakable for the same templated AI-startup site everyone else ships.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't hype** — every claim is backed by a number, a named methodology step, or a dated fact, never an adjective alone.
2. **Plainspoken over polished** — blunt, technical language; never inflate a 2-person bootstrapped company to sound bigger than it is.
3. **Compliance is a feature, not friction** — AVG/GDPR handling (cookie consent, data minimization, visitor opt-out) is demonstrated on the site itself, not just promised in a privacy policy nobody reads.
4. **Bilingual parity** — NL and EN are both first-class; nothing ships translated as an afterthought.
5. **Earn trust before asking for the call** — the honesty/about section (real founding date, real team size, real stage) is positioned before the final CTA, not buried after it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Visitor tracking/cookie consent must support a genuine opt-out (not just a dismissible banner) to satisfy AVG (the Dutch GDPR implementation), especially given the government-sector audience. Site content is bilingual NL/EN with full parity; no feature should ship in only one language.
